Known recurring transcript corrections:
- "Acme" may be mistranscribed as "acne", "hackney", or similar.
- "Carlos" may appear as "Carl" or "Karlos" due to accent-related transcription.
- "standup" may be transcribed as "stand up" — in engineering context, this refers to daily standup meetings.
- Zoom speaker labels may reflect a room device, host account, or one attendee rather than the actual speaker.
- In hybrid or room-based meetings, one labeled speaker may represent multiple in-room participants.

Apply these corrections only when supported by context. If uncertain, preserve the original and note it in the assumptions / ambiguities section.
